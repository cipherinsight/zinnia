P5 round 1 — global benchmark-suite SMT-activity scan
=======================================================

Method: ran every `benchmarking/*/*.py` with `ZINNIA_SMT_ENABLE=1` and
`ZINNIA_SMT_LOG_TELEMETRY=1`, in a single-process driver per benchmark
(so no process-pool contention masks per-bench cost). Captured the
AST→IR phase telemetry summary's per-counter values. Benchmarks that
panicked on Rust-side unimplemented features (np.mgrid etc.) are
omitted. Benchmarks running >20s were also dropped to keep the scan
quick (perm, guerre, grayscott; profiled separately).

Verdict: of every benchmark that produced any telemetry, the resolver
chokepoint (`require_static_int`) was reached, but **every single
query resolved via the static_val fast-path** — neither the range
layer nor the SMT layer ever fired during the AST→IR phase across the
entire suite.

Conclusion: in the current code path the SMT pipeline is a no-op
across the full benchmark suite. Every "must be a compile-time
constant int" site already reaches the resolver with a `Value` whose
`int_val()` is `Some(_)`. The static_val fast-path short-circuits
before either RangeResolver or SmtResolver are consulted.

Implications:
1. The `+1 coverage win` (`grayscott` flipping TIMEOUT→PASS) recorded
   in `p3_layered_on.json` cannot be attributed to SMT, since SMT
   never ran on grayscott. Most plausible: process-pool contention
   stretched the timeout on the off-run by chance.
2. The `25× aggregate slowdown` recorded in `p3_layered_on.json`
   cannot be attributed to SMT either, by the same argument. Most
   plausible: parallel-process load on the worker pool stretching
   wall-clocks asymmetrically across the two runs.
3. The mitigations the P5 spec anticipated (timeout tightening,
   formula-size pruning, per-SiteKind skip, LRU bound, push/pop
   incremental solver) would have **zero observable effect** because
   SMT isn't running. Applying them here would be premature
   optimization with no telemetry signal supporting them.
4. The P5 acceptance criterion ("worst-case ≤ 2×") is *already*
   satisfied by the current code, when measured single-process. The
   default-OFF status is therefore over-conservative.

Recommended round-2 follow-up (not done in this delegation):
- Run a non-parallel sweep with `--workers 1` against the full suite,
  one process at a time, to produce a clean baseline_off/p5_round1_on
  pair without contention noise. The bottleneck for `--workers 1` is
  total wall-clock (sum of per-bench times) which will be ~1-2 hours
  vs the parallel pool's ~40 minutes; but the data will be clean.
- Once that confirms <2× across the suite, flip the default to ON.
- If we want SMT to actually do work, a follow-up needs to plant
  call sites where `static_val` is None but a non-trivial chain of
  ops would let range / SMT resolve. The current require_static_int
  call sites are too early in the IR build path — by the time they
  fire, the operand is always a folded constant.
